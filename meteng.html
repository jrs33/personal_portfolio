MGH Metabolic Engineering

        
Merging Computer Science and Biology to 'backtrack' into liver cells to identify ideal perfusion methods

        
Description


The summer after my sophomore year I worked at MGH as a research intern. My project focused on Metabolic Engineering, which utilized both my healthcare interest, and programming abilities, and is an area of critical need to therapeutic development. In particular, the field focuses on utilizing software to take external metabolite measurements, to then backtrack into a cell to find out exactly where significant changes in the metabolic pathway are occurring.
 


Role


Specifically, I worked on a project that focused on backtracking into a liver cell to clarify the robustness of various liver perfusion methods. In otherwords, I helped some of the surgeons classify which methods of preservation were most efficient to keeping metabolic function optimal. I also was able to present my summer findings, and have a paper pending from the work I helped with.
  



Tools Used


Python, MATLAB, LaTeX
