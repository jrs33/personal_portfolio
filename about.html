About

        
Welcome to my website. Here I've compiled and centralized everything I feel represents my interests and accomplishments as an engineer. Click any of the below tabs to view a brief description about my background  and also view my downloadable resume. Enjoy!

        
            
My Story

            

 I grew up in Nashua, NH as the youngest of a family of five. Growing up, I was always attracted to math and science. Although my parents have no engineering/college background, they always pushed me to foster this interest, and I attribute a lot of my accomplishments to their support. I also was very inspired by my parents involvement in healthcare; my Mom being a hospital office manager and my Dad being a firefighter/EMT. Thus, I have found very early on that I want to use my engineering/software skills for the betterment and modernization of healthcare and health technologies.
        
            
Professional Interests

            

 Software Engineering and Entrepreneurship are what excites me. Taking an idea from the drawing board or the lab to daily-life is the most essential aspect of the engineering design process. It is where skills and creativity take form to allow for human progress. 
 
 Healthcare and health technology is a rapidly accelerating field, but still has fallen behind other industries greatly. Thus, I want to be a part of this cultural push to make healthcare catch up, whether it be through my own endeavor, or another companies who I feel embodies the same spirit and ambition as I do. 
        
            
Extra Stuff

            

I feel as though about 50% of what you learn from college comes from the classroom; the rest is on your own time. Thats why I always push to take advantage of what BU and Boston give me outside of the classroom. 

Particularly, I have gotten a bulk of my business savviness from my involvement as VP of the Entrepreneurship Club. Here, I organize events/programs, and connect with entrepreneurs all across Boston. I also work with MakeBU, which is a club that offers workshops and resources for programmers on campus. I also stay active as a BU Club Baseball member. I also am an avid learner, and partake in numerous MITOCW courses and hackathons as well.
