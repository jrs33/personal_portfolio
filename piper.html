PIPER

        
A computationally rigorous, protein-protein docking bioinformatics software

        
Description


Efficient drug design rests on sound understanding of protein-protein interactions. Thus, it is important to have efficient tools that accurately calculate the bonding energies between proteins and other general molecules. Within Professor Sandor Vajdas lab at BU, researchers have worked to develop PIPER, which functions in exactly this way, fulfilling a critical need for industry and academic researchers alike.
 


Role


Within Professor Vajda's Structural Bioinformatics Lab at BU, I have contributed, and continue to contribute as a funded student researcher to this work, and function in a team to provide constant updates, optimization, and feature addition to the robust, protein-protein interaction PIPER software.
